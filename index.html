Peculiar Floras

A Time Capsule.

Anguloa Uniflora, also known as “Swaddled babies orchid,” is produced in South America and grows during wet and dry seasons. The full growth height is from 24 to 25 inches. One of the growth requirements is to place them in greenhouses or in conditions with high humidity.
Caleana major, also known as “Large duck orchid” originates in Australia and grows during late spring to early summer. The full height of the orchid ranges from 0.5 inches to 0.75 inches. An essential growth requirement is to provide enough water supply at all times.
Cypripedium acaule, also known as “Pink lady’s slipper,” originates from New Hampshire and Maine, and usually grows from Autumn to Spring. The typical full height length is between 12 to 18 inches. Some growth requirements include placing in highly acidic soil with a range of partial shade and moisture on well-drained slopes.
Dendrophylax lindenii, also known as the “Ghost orchid” is produced from Florida and Cuba and typically grows during the summer and beginning of Fall. The average height of a full grown orchid is between 19 to 20 inches. Some of the vital requirements for sufficient growth is to supply moderately bright, filtered or scattered light with protection from direct sunlight and strong air movement is to be ensured all the time.
Drosera capensis, also known as “Cape sundew” is a carnivorous plant that originates from Cape Town, South Africa. In addition, It typically grows during the months of December and January. At full growth, the height of the plant ranges from 7 to 8 inches. Some crucial requirements for adequate growth include providing full sun to part shade and receiving ample amounts of sunlight.
Impatiens bequaertii, also known as “Dancing girls” is typically produced in various rainforests of East Africa and grows all year long. The height of the plant at full growth is about 12 inches. Some of the vital requirements for ample growth are to supply filtered light and moist, well-drained soil.
Impatiens psittacina, also known as the “Parrot flower,” originated from Southeast Asia and usually grows from October to November. The height of the flower at full growth is about 7 inches. Some of the requirements for sufficient growth are to provide a sufficient amount of shade and well-drained soil that consists of enough moisture.
Nepenthes, also known as “Pitcher plant,” is a carnivorous plant that originates in Madagascar, Southeast Asia and Australia. The full height is approximately 58.8 inches and grows from April and May. One of the growth requirements is placing the plants in bright light without much direct sunlight.
Ophrys apifera, also known as “Bee orchid,” is an orchid that is produced in the United Kingdom and grows from June to July. The full height of the flower ranges from 6 to 20 inches. Some of the requirements for sufficient growth are providing well-drained calcareous soils and placing them in bright or dim light.
Orchis italica, also known as “Naked man orchid,” is an orchid that originates in the Mediterranean Basin and grows during the months from April to May. The full height of the flower ranges from 7 to 16 inches. Some of the growth requirements are to grow in full sunlight through light shade and regular watering with a supplement of acidic and rich soil.
Pecteilis radiata, also known as the “Egret flower,” is a flower that is produced in various locations; China, Japan, Korea and Russia. The full length of the flower ranges from 19 to 20 inches, depending on the growth of each plant. A vital requirement for the flower to completely flourish is to maintain dry heat below 30 degrees celsius and to grow it in full sun to partial shade. Furthermore, the egret flower usually blooms during the months of July to August.
Rafflesia Arnoldii, also known as the “Corpse lily,” is a flower native to the rainforests of Sumatra and Borneo, both located in Indonesia. The full height of the lily is about 39.6 inches. In order for the lily to survive, it is crucial to grow in an undisturbed rainforest and for the Tetrastigma- a particular vine attached to the flower- to obtain water and nutrients. Moreover, the growth period is usually between the months from November to January, which takes approximately 3 to 4 years until it reaches its entire length.
Titan Arum, also known as the “Corpse flower,” originates in Sumatra, Indonesia. The total height of the flower is about 72 to 96 inches. Some essential requirements of the Corpse flower is to have warm growing conditions above 24 °C, in order for the flower to bloom for the first time around 5 to 10 years of vegetative growth.
Adansonia Grandidieri, also known as the “Baobab tree,” originates in Madagascar. The total height of the flower is approximately 82 to 98 feet. Some vital requirements of the Baobab tree is to have at least 6 hours of sunlight and well drained soil, to allow the tree to flourish for a maximum of 3000 years.
Araucaria araucana, also known as the “Monkey puzzle tree,” is cultivated in Southern Chile and Western Argentina and grows around 98 to 131 feet. Some requirements for ideal growth are exposure to full sunlight on well drained soil and receiving regular watering.
Dracula simia, also known as the “Monkey-like Dracula Orchid,” is produced in Ecuador, Peru, and Colombia and develops up to 12 to 24 inches. Some crucial requirements include cool or damp conditions and high humidity.
Palicourea elata, also known as the “Hooker’s lips,” originates from Central Mexico, Ecuador, and Jamaica. It grows between 3.3 to 9.8 feet with ideal conditions of a tropical environment, sunny weather and regular watering or rainfall.
